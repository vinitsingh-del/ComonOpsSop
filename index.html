Engagement Lifecycle

tap a stage to expand

The core work for CRO + Tech + Design brands — six stages, in order. The engine does the signals; the KAM owns judgement and the client.

00Intake & Brand SetupDay 0▼
  • Generate the TrooCRO magic link (7-day expiry); share over WhatsApp or email.
  • On submission the brand auto-creates — validate every field against the answers.
  • Sanity-check funnel metrics — a 65% ATC rate is a data error; reconcile first.
  • Fill KAM-only fields, confirm the hero SKU + ATC surface, add compliance, launch.
01Signal Audit & RoadmapWeek 1▼
  • Engine runs the 903-signal audit against barrier profiles B01–B43.
  • Read every signal against the brand's actual context — not in the abstract.
  • Suppress signals that don't apply — never ship noise to the client.
  • Assemble the fix roadmap, sequenced by impact.
02Competitor BenchmarkingWeek 1▼
  • Benchmark 5 Indian + 5 global competitors in the niche.
  • Score front-end, UI, and category best practices.
  • Output the benchmark matrix and the gap-to-close.
03Data Movement ReportWeek 1 → 2▼
  • Pull how the brand's data moves across the first two weeks.
  • Report against baseline — evidence, not a vanity dashboard.
  • This becomes the PDP design brief.
04PDP Design & A/B TestWeeks 2–4▼
  • Design the PDP from audit + data; 1–2 weeks of client iteration.
  • A/B test the winner: ramp 90:10 → 50:50, top 20 SKUs first.
  • Hold 95% confidence over a 28-day minimum before calling it.
  • Roll the winner across similar PDP templates.
05Page-Level Rollout & Approach PageMonth 2+▼
  • Phased: PDP → Homepage → Collection → Site-wide.
  • Resolve barriers to conversion at each template.
  • Keep the Approach Page current — facts and steps, no motivation copy.

Timeline & CVR Model

8 weeks

One brand cycle runs across 8 weeks. The 28-day test window sets the pace; from Month 3, velocity rises to 2–4 tests / month.

Month 1
Audit · Benchmark · Design
Week 1
Intake & setup, 903-signal audit, benchmarking begins.
Week 2
Benchmark delivered, data movement report, PDP design starts.
Week 3
PDP iteration — client back-and-forth.
Week 4
PDP finalised + QA. Goes live. First A/B test at 90:10.
Month 2
Test · Learn · Roll Out
Week 5
Test runs. Study heatmaps. Hold the 90:10 split.
Week 6
Ramp to 50:50. Queue test #2.
Week 7
Hits 95% confidence → winner selection.
Week 8
Roll the winner. Begin homepage. First Client Update.
CVR expectancy — lift scales with number of tests
Tests runWinners @ 60%ConservativeStrong
2 tests~1.2+4%+15%
4 tests~2.4+7%+31%
6 tests~3.6+11%+51%
8 tests~4.8+15%+74%

60% win rate; per-winner lift +3% (conservative) to +12% (strong), compounded. Expect 1–2 tests in the first 2-month cycle — Month 1 is mostly setup & design — rising to 2–4 / month from Month 3. Direction-setting estimates, not guarantees.

2

3

Brand review · ~2 weeks · three outcomes
Yes

Approved. Lock the design, close, hand off.

Changes

Iterate → re-deploy → re-review. Stay in the window.

No

Direction rejected. Back to brief, restart.

✓
Closed · signed off → Development
Edge cases
  • Guidelines missing — pause; confirm before designing.
  • No response past 2 weeks — follow up, set a deadline, then close as approved-as-is per terms.
  • Endless revisions — cap rounds, escalate to the decision-maker, scope extras as new work.
  • Conflicting feedback — route through the single named decision-maker.
  • Dev-feasibility — quick design ↔ dev check before locking.
08

AI HTML → Client Live

redesign SOP

Use this when a brand needs a fast redesign prototype in HTML before it becomes a client-approved live website. The goal is brand-safe, CRO-focused, premium-looking work that does not feel randomly generated by AI.

Standard Redesign Process:
AI HTML to Client Live

1

Brand Input Collection

Collect the brand link, guidelines, product line, page type, goals, and expected direction on call.

2

Reference & Screenshot Collection

Review the current site, logo, assets, brand colors, CRO gaps, mobile view, and full-page screenshots.

3

AI HTML Prototype Creation

Use Claude, GPT, or another AI tool to generate a mobile-and-desktop HTML prototype from the inputs.

4

Design & CRO Review

Check hierarchy, product clarity, CTA, trust, premium feel, and whether every section has a conversion reason.

5

Responsive View Check

Validate mobile, desktop, tablet, sticky CTA behavior, image crop, card stacking, and horizontal scroll.

6

GitHub Hosting & Client Review

Clean dummy content, host the preview, test the link, then share it with the named decision-maker.

7

Feedback & Revisions

Convert feedback into tasks: content, UX, CRO, layout, brand correction, or new scope. Re-review after each upload.

8

Final Approval

Get written confirmation that design, mobile, desktop, content, and scope are approved before handoff.

9

Deployment Handoff

Share the exact finalized design, assets, notes, approved link, and any implementation constraints.

10

Live Deployment

Deploy the approved design only after sign-off, then compare live vs approved preview.

11

Post-Live Monitoring

Check layout, links, CTA, tracking, speed, forms, carts, and mobile behavior after launch.

Timeline

10h
Design TimeTarget 10 hours for the first polished AI HTML preview.
1-3
Scope1 PDP, homepage, collection page, or landing page.
3d
Review TimeClient feedback should not exceed 3 days from submission.
5x
IterationsCan be up to 5 rounds if tracked and controlled.

Key Rules

  • Follow brand guidelines and original product line.
  • Do not change logo or product images unless approved.
  • Keep the design premium, brand-safe, and CRO-focused.
  • No dummy copy, random icons, emoticons, or AI-looking effects.
  • Maintain the approved design through deployment.
Short operating rule
  • Prototype is not production. AI HTML is a review artifact until cleaned, approved, and handed off.
  • Every section needs a job. Hero clarifies value, proof builds trust, FAQs remove doubt, CTA drives action.
  • Track feedback as tasks. Separate content fixes, brand fixes, CRO fixes, layout fixes, and new scope.
Approval checklist
  • Brand fit, logo, product images, colors, fonts, and tone checked.
  • Mobile, desktop, tablet, small mobile, sticky CTA, and image crops tested.
  • Preview link opens, assets load, links work, no dummy text remains.
  • Written approval confirms design, mobile, desktop, content, and deployment scope.
Edge cases trainees should catch
Guidelines or assets missingPause the design. Use the current site only as a temporary reference if the decision-maker confirms it.
AI changes the brand too muchReset around the real logo, product imagery, colors, typography, and original customer promise.
Feedback is vagueAsk for exact section, issue, desired outcome, and approval owner. Do not guess intent.
New page added mid-reviewTreat it as new scope, not a revision. Confirm timeline and price impact before building.
Live site differs from previewStop launch sign-off, compare against the approved GitHub preview, then fix before announcing live.
Tracking breaks after launchCheck analytics, pixel events, forms, checkout, CTA links, and speed before closing the task.

Figma → Dev → Deploy

zero-comeback
100% Figma deployed0 comebacks

The discipline that guarantees what ships matches the Figma exactly — no "this doesn't match," no "this is broken." Every gate must pass. Nothing skips. The brand knows every step in advance.

1
Figma locked & approvedSigned off, no further changes. Still open → not ready.
2
Gate · must passDev-readiness gateStates, responsive frames, specs, components, assets, fonts, interactions. Not ready → back to design.
3
Build to specToken-for-token. No "close enough."
4
Gate · must passQA gate — against the FigmaSide-by-side, every state + cross-device. Any miss → fix, re-QA.
5
Gate · must passStaging → brand sign-off gateBrand confirms on the staging link. Explicit written yes before live.
✓
Deploy → verified live · 100% Figma · 0 comebacks
Dev-readiness checklist
  • All states — default, hover, empty, error, loading.
  • Responsive frames — mobile + desktop.
  • Specs, components, assets at correct resolution.
  • Fonts supplied; interactions documented.
Zero-comeback rules
  • Never build from an unlocked Figma.
  • Never skip a gate — a skipped gate is a future comeback.
  • QA against the file, never from memory.
  • Written sign-off before production. Always.

Brand Figma → Live

they supply the file

When the brand hands over their own Figma. Extra validation up front — we didn't build the file — then it joins the zero-comeback process.

1

2

3
Gaps → back to the brandThey fill gaps, or sign off on assumptions in writing. Don't build on ambiguity.
4

✓

Why the extra steps
  • A brand's Figma is frequently not dev-ready — missing states, specs or responsive frames.
  • Closing gaps up front is exactly what prevents comebacks after deployment.

Escalation Section / Angry Brand

red brand protocol

Use this when the relationship is at risk: the brand is ghosting, delivery is late, deployment does not match design, the brand rejects design quality, conversions are not moving, or the brand is below ICP but expects CRO + ads scale.

Amber

Brand not responding

No reply for 7-10 days. KAM follows up, confirms blocker, and asks for decision owner.

Red

Ghosting 2-3 weeks

Mark red. Escalate internally, pause assumptions, and send a clear reactivation note.

Red

Timeline missed

Deliverable slipped versus promised SOW/timeline. Go to CTO + founding for solution path.

Red

UI/UX vs deployment mismatch

Live build does not match approved design or staging. Stop, compare, fix, then re-approve.

Design risk

Not happy with design

Route to design owner, re-read brief/SOW, isolate subjective taste vs brand/CRO mismatch.

CRO risk

Not happy with conversions

Route to CRO lead. Check baseline, test window, traffic quality, offer, data, and expectations.

Short escalation flow
1
Tag severityAmber if delayed or unclear. Red if ghosting 2-3 weeks, missed timeline, unhappy client, or live mismatch.
2
Notify the right ownerKAM owns communication. CTO owns tech/timeline/deployment. Founder owns relationship/commercial risk.
3
Run RCAWhat was promised, what slipped, why it slipped, who owns the fix, and what date is realistic.
4
Reach the brandApologise clearly, acknowledge the issue, ask for time to revert, and give a concrete next update window.
5
War-room solutionBring design/dev/CRO/ads as needed. Send one recovery plan, not scattered replies.
Whom to reach
KAM / Account ownerFirst responder. Owns client message, context, timeline, notes, apology, and follow-up cadence.
CTOMissed tech delivery, deployment mismatch, performance issue, platform blocker, or timeline recovery.
Founder / managementRed brand, churn risk, repeated misses, commercial mismatch, angry founder/client, or trust damage.
Design leadNot happy with design, UI quality, brand mismatch, Figma/AI HTML disagreement, or creative direction reset.
CRO leadConversion dissatisfaction, weak hypothesis, inconclusive test, wrong expectation, or funnel diagnosis issue.
Ads / growth ownerClient expects CRO + ads, traffic quality affects CVR, spend/revenue mismatch, or ICP check required.
ICP gate for CRO + ads brands

If a brand needs CRO + ads but is below ICP, do not promise a full-scale growth path without leadership review. Route to founder/commercial discussion before committing.

Revenue ICPMinimum INR 30 lakhs revenue per month.
Ad spend ICPMinimum INR 5 lakhs ad spend per month.
If below ICPFlag as commercial risk. Founder + ads/growth owner decide fit, scope, and expectation.
RCA

RCA rule: for red brands, document the promise, the miss, the root cause, team owner, recovery ETA, client message, and prevention step. Then send one apology-led update to the brand: acknowledge, apologise, ask for time to revert, and return with a solution.

The steps, in order
  • Onboard & research (1 day, 2 hrs). Explain Troopod + expectations. Register in the brand DB.
  • Categorize & route by scope — mind the 40-hour gate.
  • Create SOW: classify work, audit current gaps, build task table, approve scope, route to lifecycle.
  • Pick the journey — CRO-led or Build-first.
  • Run the lifecycle: intake → audit → benchmark → data → PDP + A/B → rollout.
  • For AI HTML redesigns, clean the prototype, review CRO, host preview, control revisions, approve, deploy, monitor.
  • Design/dev passes every gate — 100% Figma, 0 comebacks.
  • If brand risk appears, run escalation: severity tag → owner → RCA → apology → recovery plan.
  • Track the 2-month timeline; CVR compounds as you run more tests.
Key points to remember
  • Research first so categorization has 0 issues.
  • SOW before delivery — no execution without approved scope, hours, timeline, and commercials.
  • Explain every step to the brand in advance.
  • Suppress signals that don't apply before sharing.
  • A/B: 60% win · 95% confidence · 28-day min · top 20 SKUs.
  • AI HTML is a prototype until cleaned, approved, and handed off.
  • Never build from an unlocked Figma; never skip a gate.
  • Red brands go to CTO + founder/management with RCA and a single recovery plan.
  • New brand + 40+ hrs tech → build-first.
  • Page Optimization POC = CTO. Lighthouse is lab; Google ranks on CrUX.
  • Both journeys end in CRO retention. North-star = Conversion Rate.
